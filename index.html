Section 2 of 4: Importance

How important is each factor to your organisation?

Rate how important each factor is when choosing a credentialing platform. Use a scale of 1 (not important) to 5 (critical). You can give multiple factors the same rating.

Brand Awareness
How well-known and trusted the platform is among institutions, employers, and earners. A recognized platform adds credibility to the credentials your organization issues.
Not importantCritical
Analytics
The quality and usefulness of data issuers can access about how their credentials are being used. This includes sharing rates, employer views, and recipient engagement, as well as whether the reporting tools give you a real picture of program performance.
Not importantCritical
Security
How confident you are that the platform protects your data and meets your IT and security requirements. This includes credential verification, fraud prevention, and compliance with your organization's policies.
Not importantCritical
Ease of Use (Earner)
How easy it is for earners to receive, view, and share their credentials. This covers the claim process, sharing to LinkedIn and other platforms, and the overall experience from the earner's side.
Not importantCritical
Ease of Use (Issuer)
How straightforward it is to set up, manage, and issue credentials at scale. This covers the admin interface, bulk issuance, integrations with your existing tools, and the day-to-day admin effort.
Not importantCritical
Customization
How much flexibility you have to tailor credentials to your brand and program needs. This includes visual design, badge templates, metadata fields, and alignment with your organization's standards.
Not importantCritical
Customer Support
How helpful and responsive the vendor is when you need assistance, especially during setup and when problems come up. This covers documentation quality, response times, and the overall working relationship.
Not importantCritical
